Music Theory:

Music theory is the study of how music works. It includes learning about notes, scales, chords, rhythm, and how they all fit together to create songs. Understanding music theory helps musicians read and write music, communicate with others, and improve their playing. It gives a clear structure to music, making it easier to create and perform with confidence. Whether you're writing a song or playing an instrument, music theory helps you understand what you're doing and why it sounds good.

Ear Training:

Ear training is the practice of learning to hear and recognize musical elements, such as notes, intervals, chords, and rhythms. It helps musicians develop a strong musical ear, which is important for playing by ear, tuning instruments, and singing or playing in tune. With good ear training, you can better understand what you hear and respond to it quickly. This skill is especially useful when playing with others or learning music without sheet music. Ear training makes you a more confident and skilled musician.

Music Theory:

Music Theory and Practice Info

Source: Glenn McMahon

This resource provides an introduction to core music theory concepts. It outlines the three main elements of music: Melody, which is a sequence of single notes forming a tune, Harmony, created when multiple notes sound together, typically as chords, and Rhythm, a repeated pattern of sound.

Understanding notes and their relationships is key. Music uses 12 notes, and a Scale is a specific set of notes used in a piece, defining its Key. Major keys often sound happy, while minor keys sound sad. The Circle of Fourths and Fifths is a tool for understanding key signatures and the relationships between keys.

The resource also covers Music Notation, explaining how pitch, duration, and rhythm are written using staves, clefs, key signatures, time signatures, notes, and rests. Concepts like Intervals (distance between notes), Cadences (chord endings), and different Chord Types are also explored.
